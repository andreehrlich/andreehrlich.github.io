Epidemic Forecasting in Brazil: Latent log-GP for Time-Varying Transmission.

About

This project concerns the modeling of infectious diseases and in particular with assessing the predictive ability of age-stratified viral load data on the disease reproduction number and related transmission dynamics. We also assess the inclusion of PCR viral load measurements towards large-scale surveillance. Epidemics are difficult to model due to sparse, noisey and biased observation process and the underlying infection structure. We model the time-varying transmission with a latent log-Gaussian Process prior within a hierarchical stochastic epidemic model. We evaluate viral load data from Brazil, which has the world's largest government-run public health system serving 200M+ people.
